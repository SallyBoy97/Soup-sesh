Katsu

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Chicken katsu is a Japanese dish made with breaded and fried chicken cutlets. It is typically served with a dipping sauce, such as tonkatsu sauce or tartar sauce. Chicken katsu is a popular dish in Japan and can be found at many restaurants and cafes. It is also a popular dish to make at home.

Instructions

  1. Cut the chicken breasts into 1-inch thick strips.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg and milk.
  4. Dip the chicken strips into the egg mixture, then coat them in the flour mixture.
  5. Heat the oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  6. Fry the chicken strips for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Serve the chicken katsu with your favorite dipping sauce.
